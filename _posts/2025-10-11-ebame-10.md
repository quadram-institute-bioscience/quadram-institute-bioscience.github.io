Andrea Telatin and Evelien Adriaenssens returned to Brest, France, for the **10th EBAME Workshop on Computational Microbial Ecogenomics** (October 11–25, 2025), delivering an updated hands-on viromics tutorial.

<!--more-->

![EBAME 2025]({{ site.baseurl }}/images/photos/ebame-vertical-2025.jpg)

Our tutorial — [**Mining phage sequences from metagenomes**](https://quadram-institute-bioscience.github.io/ebame-viromics/) — walks participants through a full viromics workflow: identifying viral sequences in metagenomic assemblies, backmapping reads to estimate viral abundance, building abundance tables, and visualising results with Anvi'o. An optional module covers NextFlow-based automation for scaling the analysis.

The material is openly available under a CC BY-SA 4.0 licence, and contributions are welcome via the [GitHub repository](https://github.com/quadram-institute-bioscience/ebame-viromics).
